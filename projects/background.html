New York Times The New York Times article titled “Yes, Looks Do Matter,” discusses the reasons as to why we as humans, have a tendency to judge others based on their looks. According to the article, the basis of this trait originated back when we were hunter-gatherers and cave-dwellers, where we needed to look at something and be able to identify what it was and what its intentions were, in a matter of seconds. Physical attractiveness can be tied to higher social adeptness, high intelligence levels, and strong leadership capabilities, even if the person in question has none of the aforementioned abilities. Stanford Research Nature's Benefits In the Stanford article, researchers found the cure to poor mental state and low mental health--nature.
